# M&A Regulatory Review (并购监管审核) — Comprehensive Reference

> **Purpose:** Reference for PRC regulatory review processes and requirements covering major asset restructurings, CSRC review, antitrust clearance, national security review, and foreign investment compliance.
> **Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## 1. Major Asset Restructuring (MAR) Determination (重大资产重组判定)

### 1.1 Statutory Thresholds (《重组办法》第12条)

A transaction constitutes a **Major Asset Restructuring (重大资产重组)** if any one of the following three ratios reaches **50%** or more:

| Ratio | Formula | Numerator | Denominator |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Asset Ratio (资产总额比)** | Total Assets of Target / Total Assets of Listed Co. | Total assets of acquired/sold entity (at transaction benchmark date); higher of book value and transaction value | Total assets of listed company (most recent fiscal year-end, audited consolidated) |
| **Revenue Ratio (营业收入比)** | Revenue of Target / Revenue of Listed Co. | Revenue of acquired/sold entity (latest fiscal year) | Revenue of listed company (latest fiscal year, audited consolidated) |
| **Net Asset Ratio (净资产比)** | Net Assets of Target / Net Assets of Listed Co. | Net assets of acquired/sold entity (at transaction benchmark date); higher of book value and transaction value | Net assets of listed company (latest fiscal year-end, audited consolidated) |

**Key Clarifications (2023 CSRC Revision):**

| Scenario | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Multiple targets within 12 months** | Aggregate ratios apply; transactions with same counterparty or related parties counted cumulatively |
| **Acquisition of minority stake** | Ratio based on total target (not pro-rata % acquired) if acquiring control |
| **Cash acquisitions** | Subject to MAR determination; MAR status triggers CSRC review regardless of payment form |
| **Asset ratio ≥ 100% AND asset/NAV ≥ RMB 500M** | Constitutes "exceptional MAR" (特别重大资产重组) — triggers IPO-level review by M&A Committee |
| **12-month look-back** | Transactions involving same or related assets in preceding 12 months aggregated for ratio calculation |

### 1.2 Review Pathways Based on MAR Classification

| Classification | Review Body | Timeline | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Non-MAR (<50% all ratios)** | Exchange-only review or disclosure-based | 1–3 months | Board approval + shareholder vote (simple majority); no CSRC approval needed (informational disclosure) |
| **MAR — Cash (≥50%, cash payment)** | Exchange review (轻审核) | 2–4 months | Board + shareholder vote (2/3 majority); exchange inquiry process |
| **MAR — Shares (≥50%, share issuance)** | CSRC M&A Committee Review | 6–9 months | Board + shareholder vote (2/3); independent financial advisor; asset appraisal report; CSRC approval |
| **Exceptional MAR (≥100% + ≥RMB 500M)** | CSRC M&A Committee (enhanced) | 8–12 months | IPO-level scrutiny; enhanced DD; restructuring committee deliberation |

### 1.3 MAR Negative List (不得重组情形)

Under current CSRC rules, MAR is prohibited if the listed company:
1. Is under CSRC investigation for suspected illegal activities
2. Has been subject to public censure by the exchange within the past 12 months
3. Has audit opinion with disclaimer, adverse opinion, or qualified opinion for material uncertainty
4. Has controlling shareholder or actual controller with material rule violations under investigation
5. The target assets have material ownership defects, unresolved disputes, or lack of proper title
6. The restructuring would result in the acquirer not meeting listing qualification standards

## 2. CSRC / Exchange Review Process (证监会审核流程)

### 2.1 Standard Review Timeline

```
Stage 1: PRE-FILING (1–2 months before acceptance)
  └─ Independent Financial Advisor preliminary review
  └─ Due diligence completion
  └─ Board resolution approving restructuring plan
  └─ Shareholder meeting resolution (2/3 special majority)
  └─ Draft filing documents prepared

Stage 2: ACCEPTANCE (受理) — ~10 business days
  ├─ Submit application to CSRC (or designated exchange)
  ├─ CSRC preliminary form review (5 business days)
  ├─ Acceptance notice issued (受理通知书) OR rejection with reasons
  └─ Public disclosure of restructuring summary on CSRC website

Stage 3: PRELIMINARY REVIEW (初审) — 20–45 business days
  ├─ CSRC review team examines filing documents
  ├─ Professional feedback (反馈意见) issued (Phase 1)
  ├─ Applicant response (≤ 30 business days; extendable once)
  ├─ Phase 2 feedback if needed (≤ 15 business days)
  ├─ Professional review opinion issued
  └─ Case transferred to M&A Committee

Stage 4: M&A COMMITTEE DELIBERATION (并购重组委审议) — 15–30 business days
  ├─ Committee meeting scheduled (notice ≥ 5 business days prior)
  ├─ Applicant, financial advisor, legal counsel, auditor attend hearing
  ├─ Committee Q&A (1–3 hours)
  ├─ Deliberation & secret ballot vote
  └─ Result: Approved / Conditionally Approved (TIA) / Rejected

Stage 5: APPROVAL (核准) — ~10 business days
  ├─ Post-committee approval documentation
  ├─ CSRC official approval notice (核准批复)
  ├─ Approval valid for 12 months (may extend 6 months)
  └─ Implementation must begin within validity period

       TOTAL TYPICAL: 6–9 months (straightforward cases)
```

### 2.2 Key Participants & Responsibilities

| Participant | Role | Key Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| **Listed Company (上市公司)** | Applicant; primary responsible party | Restructuring report (重组报告书); board resolutions; shareholder meeting notice |
| **Independent Financial Advisor (独立财务顾问)** | Gatekeeper; lead advisor; continuing supervision | IFA opinion letter (独立财务顾问报告); due diligence working papers; ongoing supervision reports |
| **Legal Counsel (法律顾问)** | Legal due diligence; compliance assurance | Legal opinion letter (法律意见书); legal DD report |
| **Auditor (审计机构)** | Financial DD; audit opinions | Audit report (审计报告); pro-forma financials; internal control attestation |
| **Asset Appraiser (评估机构)** | Valuation/appraisal | Appraisal report (评估报告); appraisal methodology explanation |
| **Target Company** | Cooperative party; info provider | Financial statements; business description; material contract disclosure |

### 2.3 Review Focus Areas by Regulator

| Focus Area | Detailed Scope |
|---|---|
| **Transaction Necessity** | Strategic rationale; synergy quantification; alternatives analysis |
| **Pricing Fairness** | Appraisal methodology; comparable analysis; premium justification |
| **Performance Commitment (VAM)** | Realism of targets; enforceability; historical performance track record |
| **Ongoing Listing Qualifications** | Post-restructuring financial metrics meet listing standards; no new going-concern issues |
| **Related-Party Transactions** | Independent director opinion; connected transaction avoidance; fairness opinion |
| **Information Disclosure** | Completeness; consistency across reports; material risk disclosure |
| **Shareholder Protection** | Dissenting shareholder cash-out option; appraisal-based fairness; procedural compliance |
| **Post-Restructuring Governance** | Board independence; management stability; related-party transaction controls |
| **Target Business Sustainability** | Client/supplier concentration; IP ownership; regulatory licenses; key person dependency |
| **Financial Integrity** | Revenue recognition; related-party transactions; tax compliance; contingent liabilities |

## 3. M&A Committee Review (并购重组委)

### 3.1 Committee Composition & Voting

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Total Members** | 35–40 (drawn from legal, accounting, finance, industry experts, academics) |
| **Per-Review Panel** | 5 members (minimum 3 present for quorum) |
| **Voting** | Secret ballot; ≥3 votes for approval; ≥3 votes for rejection |
| **Recusals** | Members with conflict of interest recused (past 2-year relationship with parties) |
| **Term** | 1 year; renewable for up to 2 consecutive terms |

### 3.2 Conditional Approval (有条件通过 / TIA)

| Condition Type | Typical Requirement | Compliance Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Supplementary Disclosure** | Additional risk disclosure or financial breakdown | Before implementation |
| **Amended VAM Terms** | Adjusted performance targets or compensation mechanism | Within 30 days |
| **Post-Closing Undertaking** | Specific compliance actions post-restructuring | Within 6–12 months |
| **Shareholder Protection Enhancement** | Improved dissenting shareholder buyback price | Before shareholder vote |

### 3.3 Rejection Triggers — Top 10 Reasons

| # | Rejection Reason | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Inadequate sustainability of target's profitability — excessive reliance on non-recurring items or single customers | High |
| 2 | Appraisal assumptions unreasonable — DCF growth rate unjustified; comparable companies not truly comparable | High |
| 3 | Failure to demonstrate pricing fairness — deal consideration not supported by independent valuation | Medium |
| 4 | Material information disclosure deficiencies — inconsistency or omission | Medium |
| 5 | Target business model or industry outlook uncertain — regulatory/policy risk unmitigated | Medium |
| 6 | Related-party transaction concerns — potential tunneling or unfair diversion of benefits | Medium |
| 7 | VAM target unrealistic — historical performance does not support committed growth trajectory | Medium |
| 8 | Post-restructuring governance concerns — controlling shareholder dominance; lack of independence | Low |
| 9 | Material legal/compliance issues with target — unresolved litigation; IP disputes; license gaps | Low |
| 10 | Inadequate due diligence — IFA DD quality below standard | Low |

## 4. Private Fund Filing (私募基金备案)

### 4.1 When Filing is Required

Private equity/venture capital funds participating as **transaction counterparties** (as acquirer, seller, or financing party) in MAR transactions must demonstrate compliance with AMAC (Asset Management Association of China) filing requirements:

| Fund Type | Requirement | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **PE/VC Fund as Acquirer** | Must be AMAC-registered and fund-filed before CSRC filing | Before MAR application |
| **PE/VC Fund as Seller** | Must demonstrate AMAC registration; no pending disciplinary actions | Before CSRC filing |
| **PE/VC Fund as Capital Provider** (PIPE) | Registration and filing required if controlling stake involved | Before CSRC filing |
| **Fund Manager** | Must be AMAC-registered; no material non-compliance record | Concurrently with fund filing |

### 4.2 Documentation for MAR Filing

| Document | Issued By | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| **AMAC Registration Certificate** | AMAC | Fund manager registration number; fund filing number |
| **Compliance Confirmation Letter** | Fund legal counsel | No ongoing AMAC investigation; no disciplinary record |
| **Fund Structure Disclosure** | Fund manager | LP structure; leverage; investment committee composition; key person clause |
| **Fund Duration vs. Lock-Up** | Fund counsel | Fund life remaining ≥ lock-up period for shares received + 1 year |

## 5. Foreign Investor M&A — National Security Review (外国投资者并购安全审查)

### 5.1 Triggering Scope

Under the **PRC Foreign Investment Law (外商投资法, 2020)** and **Measures for Security Review of Foreign Investment (外商投资安全审查办法, 2021)**:

| Triggering Transaction | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Acquisition of control** of a PRC enterprise by foreign investor(s) | All industries; mandatory if military/defense-related |
| **Significant influence** in "Important" sectors | Agriculture, energy, resources, infrastructure, transport, cultural services, IT & internet, financial services, key technology |
| **Acquisition of "Important IP" or "Important trade secrets"** | In protected industry sectors |
| **Greenfield with significant market share impact** | In "Important" sectors listed above |

### 5.2 Review Process

```
Foreign Investor files with NDRC/ MOFCOM Working Office
  ↓ (15 business days)
Working Office decision: Not Subject to Review / General Review / Special Review
  ↓
General Review (30 business days):
  └─ Office assesses impact on national security
  └─ Decision: Pass / Enter Special Review
  ↓
Special Review (60 business days):
  └─ Consultations with military, industry regulators, intelligence agencies
  └─ Cabinet-level deliberation for sensitive cases
  ↓
Decision:
  ├─ Approved (Pass)
  ├─ Conditionally Approved (with Mitigation Measures)
  └─ Rejected (Prohibited)
```

### 5.3 Mitigation Measures (Conditional Approval)

| Measure | Example |
|---|---|
| **Ring-Fencing** | Separate governance; localized data storage; independent board with PRC citizen majority |
| **Asset/Entity Carve-Out** | Divest sensitive business lines or assets pre-closing |
| **Operational Restriction** | Limits on access to sensitive data, technology, or facilities |
| **Reporting Obligation** | Periodic compliance reporting to review authority |
| **Government Veto / Golden Share** | Government retains approval rights for material decisions |

## 6. Antitrust Review — Merger Control (反垄断审查 / 经营者集中申报)

### 6.1 Notification Thresholds (国务院令第773号, 2024)

**Mandatory filing required** if **both** thresholds met:

| Threshold | Before 2024 | After 2024 Revision |
|---|---|---|
| **Global Revenue (combined)** | ≥ RMB 10B | ≥ RMB 12B |
| **PRC Revenue (combined)** | ≥ RMB 2B | ≥ RMB 2.4B |
| **Individual PRC Revenue** | ≥ RMB 400M each (≥2 parties) | ≥ RMB 480M each (≥2 parties) |

**Alternative "Small Transaction" Threshold:**
Even if above met, **no notification required** if:
- Combined global revenue < RMB 12B **AND** combined PRC revenue < RMB 800M
  — OR —
- Combined global revenue < RMB 12B **AND** individual PRC revenue of each < RMB 800M

### 6.2 Review Phases (SAMR — State Administration for Market Regulation)

| Phase | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Filing Consultation** | 1–2 months | Voluntary; discuss case complexity, information requirements with SAMR |
| **Formal Acceptance (立案)** | ~1–2 weeks | SAMR reviews filing for completeness; may reject and request resubmission |
| **Phase I Review** | 30 calendar days | Routine review for non-problematic cases; outcome: Clearance or Enter Phase II |
| **Phase II Review** | 90 calendar days (+ 60 days extension) | Detailed competitive assessment for complex/problematic cases |
| **"Stop the Clock"** | Unlimited (new 2024 rule) | SAMR may suspend the review clock for information requests; common in complex cases |
| **Outcome** | — | Unconditional Clearance / Conditional Clearance (with Remedies) / Prohibition |

### 6.3 SAMR Competitive Assessment Framework

| Factor | Analysis |
|---|---|
| **Market Definition** | Relevant product market; geographic market (national/provincial/local) |
| **Market Concentration** | HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index); CR4 concentration ratio |
| **Unilateral Effects** | Post-merger ability to raise prices / reduce output / reduce innovation |
| **Coordinated Effects** | Risk of tacit collusion post-merger |
| **Non-Horizontal Effects** | Vertical foreclosure; conglomerate effects (portfolio power) |
| **Countervailing Factors** | Market entry analysis; buyer power; efficiencies defense; failing firm defense |
| **National Interest** | Industrial policy considerations (implicit in PRC review) |

### 6.4 Remedies in Conditional Clearance

| Remedy Type | Description | Recent Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Structural — Divestiture** | Sell overlapping business/assets to approved buyer | Semiconductor acquisitions; pharma overlaps |
| **Structural — IP Licensing** | License key IP/technology to competitors on FRAND terms | Tech sector mergers |
| **Behavioral — FRAND Commitment** | Non-discriminatory supply/ licensing | Infrastructure mergers; platform deals |
| **Behavioral — Firewall / Chinese Wall** | Restrict information sharing between businesses | Vertical mergers in data-sensitive sectors |
| **Behavioral — Non-Discrimination** | Equal treatment of downstream competitors | Vertical platform deals |
| **Behavioral — Hold-Separate** | Maintain separate operations pending final determination | Interim measures |
| **Hybrid** | Combination of structural + behavioral | Complex multi-market deals |

### 6.5 Gun-Jumping & Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| **Failure to Notify** | Fine up to 10% of preceding FY group revenue; order to restore competitive conditions |
| **Gun-Jumping (Closing Before Clearance)** | Fine up to 10% of revenue; transactions may be voided |
| **Providing False Information** | Fine up to 5% of revenue for companies; RMB 500K for individuals |
| **Violating Conditions/Remedies** | Fine up to 10% of revenue; revocation of clearance decision |

## 7. Common Review Focus Points — Top 15 (常见审核关注点)

| # | Focus Point | Why It Matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sustainability of Target Profitability** | CSRC wants post-restructuring earnings stability — not one-time bump | Detailed market analysis; customer diversification plan; competitive moat description |
| 2 | **Valuation Reasonableness** | Appraisal premium vs. book value must be justified | Multi-method valuation; comparable transactions Table; sensitivity analysis |
| 3 | **Performance Commitment (VAM) Realism** | Historic growth rate vs. committed CAGR gap | Bridge analysis; industry growth benchmark; specific growth drivers detailed |
| 4 | **Related-Party Transactions Post-Closing** | Risk of tunneling / unfair value transfer | Internal control mechanism; independent director oversight; arm's length pricing commitment |
| 5 | **Target's Asset Ownership / Title** | Core assets must be free of encumbrances | Title verification; IP registration; land use rights; patent validity |
| 6 | **Customer / Supplier Concentration** | >50% revenue from top 5 customers raises going-concern concern | Diversification plan; long-term contract disclosure; switching cost analysis |
| 7 | **Key Person Dependency** | If CEO/founder is critical to target operations | Retention agreement; succession plan; management bench strength description |
| 8 | **Tax Compliance History** | Unresolved tax audits may delay approval | Tax clearance certificate; tax counsel opinion; historical tax compliance table |
| 9 | **Environmental / Safety Compliance** | Particularly for manufacturing, chemical, mining targets | Environmental impact assessment; compliance certificates; no major violation record |
| 10 | **Ongoing Litigation / Arbitration** | Material pending cases may derail valuation | Full disclosure; reserve adequacy analysis; legal counsel opinion on likely outcomes |
| 11 | **Regulatory / License Validity** | Industries requiring government licenses (pharma, telecom, finance) | License status table; renewal prospects; regulatory communication record |
| 12 | **Post-Restructuring Governance** | Board composition; independent director ratio; committee structure | Governance plan; nominee CVs; no concentration of power |
| 13 | **Information Disclosure Consistency** | Discrepancies between filings trigger credibility concerns | Cross-document consistency check; unified data source |
| 14 | **Minority Shareholder Protection** | Dissenting shareholder rights; cumulative voting; appraisal rights | Cash-out option at fair value; shareholder communication plan |
| 15 | **Historical Fund-Raising Use of Proceeds** | Regulator checks if prior IPO/refinancing proceeds properly used | Use-of-proceeds report; bank verification; no material deviation from disclosed plan |

## 8. Restructuring Plan Amendment & Re-Application (方案调整与二次申报)

### 8.1 Amendment Triggers

| Trigger | Amendment Requirement | Process |
|---|---|---|
| **Transaction counterparty change** | Major amendment — restart review | Re-file; new acceptance process |
| **Target asset scope change (≥20%)** | Form 2 amendment — additional review period | Supplementary filing; Committee reconsideration |
| **Valuation change (≥20%)** | Form 2 amendment | Revised appraisal; supplementary disclosure |
| **Payment structure change** (cash vs. shares ratio) | Form 1 amendment — shorter review | Supplementary filing; no committee re-hearing unless material |
| **VAM terms change** | Form 1 or Form 2 depending on magnitude | Revised performance commitment agreement |
| **Pricing adjustment (≤20%)** | Form 1 amendment | Price adjustment mechanism disclosure |
| **Change in financing arrangement** | Form 1 amendment | Revised financing plan; commitment letters |
| **Material adverse change in target business** | Major amendment — restart review | Re-evaluate entire transaction viability |

### 8.2 Re-Application after Rejection

| Scenario | Waiting Period | Required Changes |
|---|---|---|
| **CSRC Formal Rejection** | No mandatory waiting period; but must address reasons | New board resolution; updated financials; addressed rejection reasons |
| **Withdrawn & Re-filed** | Typically 3–6 months due to preparation needs | Materially revised plan; updated DD; new IFA opinion |
| **M&A Committee Rejection** | Same as CSRC formal rejection | Committee rejection reasons must be specifically addressed |
| **Expired Approval** (validity lapsed) | Any time; treat as new application | Updated financials to latest period; re-verified DD |

### 8.3 "Quick Re-File" Scenarios

| Change | Can Use Quick Re-File? | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Updated financial period only (no other changes) | Yes — supplementary filing | Auditor's report for new period; no material adverse change |
| Minor VAM adjustment (within original spirit) | Yes — supplementary filing | ≤20% adjustment to performance targets; same compensation mechanism |
| Change in intermediary (new IFA / law firm) | Yes — need new opinion letters | New intermediary must be qualified; new opinion letters replacing prior |
| Non-controlling shareholder change in acquirer consortium | Yes — disclosure update | No change to controlling shareholder or actual controller |

### 8.4 Practical Tips for Amendment / Re-Application

1. **Understand the real reason for rejection** — beyond the official notice; informal channel feedback often more insightful
2. **Change the narrative, not just the numbers** — strategic story must address regulator's substantive concern
3. **Consider changing financial advisor** — a fresh IFA signals a genuinely improved application to the regulator
4. **Time the market window** — certain periods (policy windows, political cycles) more favorable for approvals
5. **Pre-file consultation** — CSRC staff may give informal guidance on whether amendment would be approvable before formal re-commitment
6. **Consider alternative structures** — if MAR path is blocked, consider: (a) minority investment, (b) joint venture, (c) deferred acquisition with call option

For cross-border deals, consider parallel filing strategy:

| Jurisdiction | Filing Trigger | Review Period | Coordination |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PRC (SAMR)** | See §6.1 above | 1.5–8 months | File simultaneously with EU/US |
