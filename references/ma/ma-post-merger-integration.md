# Post-Merger Integration (并购后整合) — Comprehensive Reference

> **Purpose:** Framework for planning, executing, and monitoring post-merger integration (PMI) covering strategic, organizational, financial, operational, IT, and HR dimensions.
> **Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## 1. Integration Philosophy & Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Speed Over Perfection** | Integration momentum must begin before Day 0; uncertainty is the enemy of value capture |
| **Culture First** | Cultural incompatibility causes >50% of integration failures — address early, address often |
| **Retain Key Talent** | Top performers leave fastest; golden handcuffs + clear career paths are essential |
| **Customer Continuity** | Zero disruption to key customer relationships during transition |
| **One Version of Truth** | Single integration dashboard; single data model; single reporting cadence |
| **Empowered Integration Team** | Dedicated full-time IMO (Integration Management Office) with C-suite mandate |

## 3. Integration Dimensions (整合维度)

### 3.1 Strategic Integration

| Component | Key Questions | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| **Vision Alignment** | What is the combined entity's strategic narrative? | Strategy document (5-pager) |
| **Portfolio Rationalization** | Which overlapping products/geographies to consolidate? | Portfolio review matrix |
| **Brand Strategy** | House of brands vs. branded house? | Brand architecture decision |
| **Innovation Roadmap** | Combined R&D pipeline; resource allocation | Integrated product roadmap |
| **Market Positioning** | Combined market share; pricing power assessment | Competitive positioning deck |

### 3.2 Organizational Integration

| Component | Approach | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Leadership Selection** | Merit-based + retention-driven; external assessment center for top 30 roles | Day 1–30 |
| **Organization Design** | Delayering; span-of-control optimization (target: 1:6–1:8) | Day 30–60 |
| **Reporting Lines** | Minimize matrix complexity; clear accountability | Day 30–75 |
| **Headcount Rationalization** | Transparent criteria; voluntary separation program first | Day 60–90 |
| **Decision Rights** | RACI for top 50 decisions; delegation of authority framework | Day 45–75 |

**Organization Design Principles:**
1. Accountabilities are singular and unambiguous
2. Spans of control are manageable (manager:staff = 1:6–8)
3. Layers from CEO to frontline ≤ 5
4. Functional reporting into business units with dotted-line to corporate
5. Integration team roles sunset within 12 months

### 3.3 Financial Integration

| Component | Detailed Activities | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Chart of Accounts Harmonization** | Map both COAs; design combined COA; test with parallel run | Day 30–90 |
| **Financial Systems** | Consolidation system (Hyperion / SAP BPC / Tagetik); single ERP decision | Day 60–180 |
| **Treasury** | Cash pooling; bank relationship consolidation; FX exposure hedging alignment | Day 1–30 |
| **Planning & Budgeting** | Combined annual budget; forecasting cadence (monthly rolling forecast) | Day 60–120 |
| **Management Reporting** | Combined P&L by business unit; synergy tracker; one version of truth | Day 30–60 |
| **Internal Controls** | SOX/CSOX alignment; segregation of duties review; approval matrix update | Day 90–180 |

### 3.5 IT Integration

| Phase | Focus | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 0 — Triage (Day 1–7)** | Must-run systems | Ensure network connectivity; email routing; security perimeter |
| **Phase 1 — Quick Wins (Day 8–60)** | User productivity | Active Directory merge; single sign-on; collaboration tools; file share access |
| **Phase 2 — Rationalization (Day 60–180)** | Application portfolio | Application inventory; redundancy identification; target architecture decision |

**IT Integration Budget Rule of Thumb:** 3%–5% of combined entity revenue in Year 1; 1.5%–2% post-stabilization.

## 5. Integration Risk & Mitigation (整合风险清单)

### 5.1 Risk Register

| Risk ID | Risk Description | Probability | Impact | Severity | Mitigation Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **R-01** | Key talent flight (top management / critical engineers) | High | Critical | 🔴 25 | Retention agreements (18–24 mo); accelerated vesting; career path clarity; stay interviews monthly |
| **R-02** | Customer attrition due to uncertainty | Medium | Critical | 🔴 20 | Dedicated account coverage; joint customer calls Day 1; "business as usual" pricing guarantee |
| **R-03** | Cultural clash / "us vs. them" dynamics | High | High | 🟠 16 | Culture diagnostic pre-close; joint leadership offsite; mixed teams; shared values campaign |
| **R-04** | IT system integration delays / data migration failures | Medium | High | 🟠 12 | Independent IT due diligence; dedicated data migration team; parallel run before cutover |
| **R-05** | Synergy over-estimation — integration costs exceed benefits | Medium | Critical | 🟠 15 | Stretch vs. base case synergy model; integration cost contingency (15%); monthly synergy tracking |
| **R-06** | Regulatory / antitrust conditions force divestitures | Low | High | 🟡 8 | Pre-close buyer identification for possible forced divestitures; ring-fenced operations |
| **R-07** | Day 1 operational disruption (payroll, billing, supply chain) | Low | Critical | 🟡 6 | Dry runs; parallel processing; manual fallback plans; extended support hours |
| **R-08** | Loss of key suppliers / supplier renegotiation risk | Medium | Medium | 🟡 6 | Supplier communications Day 1; contract continuity letters; advance supply agreements |
| **R-09** | Brand confusion / market positioning conflict | Medium | Medium | 🟡 6 | Brand architecture study pre-close; phased migration; customer communication campaign |
| **R-10** | Integration fatigue — team burnout after 6+ months | High | Medium | 🟡 8 | Rotate IMO team members; celebration of milestones; workload balancing; external surge support |
| **R-11** | Compliance gaps — SOX/regulatory control breakdown during transition | Low | Critical | 🟡 8 | Control owner continuity; interim control testing; compliance officer embedded in IMO |
| **R-12** | Real estate / facility rationalization delays | Medium | Low | 🟢 4 | Early lease review; flexible workspace adoption; temporary co-location |
| **R-13** | Foreign exchange risk from cross-border integration costs | Medium | Low | 🟢 3 | Hedging program; local-currency cost allocation; natural hedging where possible |
| **R-14** | Intellectual property leakage / data security breach | Low | Critical | 🟡 8 | Clean team protocol pre-close; access controls Day 1; cyber security assessment; IP registry update |
| **R-15** | Governance deadlock — unclear decision-making authority | Medium | Medium | 🟡 6 | Decision rights matrix pre-close; escalation protocol; integration steering committee weekly |

### 5.2 Risk Monitoring Framework

| Tier | Response | Review Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **🔴 Critical (20+)** | Immediate escalation to Steering Committee; CEO awareness; dedicated action team with daily stand-ups | Daily until downgraded |
| **🟠 High (10–19)** | IMO Lead ownership; specific mitigation plan with weekly milestones; Steering Committee aware | Weekly |
| **🟡 Medium (4–9)** | Workstream lead ownership; mitigation tracked in status dashboard | Bi-weekly |
| **🟢 Low (1–3)** | Monitored; no active mitigation unless probability increases | Monthly |

## 6. Common Integration Failure Analysis (常见整合失败原因)

### 6.1 Root Cause Taxonomy

| Category | Failure Mode | Incidence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategic** | Over-estimation of synergies (winner's curse) | 25–30% | Independent synergy validation; 3-point estimates (optimistic/base/pessimistic) |
| **Strategic** | Strategic misfit — acquired business fundamentally incompatible | 15–20% | Enhanced strategic DD; red-team challenge of thesis |
| **Cultural** | Cultural incompatibility underestimated | 40–50% | Culture DD; integration planning for culture from pre-signing |
| **Cultural** | "Conqueror syndrome" — acquirer imposes culture without listening | 20–25% | Reverse mentoring; best-of-both approach; cultural integration sub-committee |
| **Talent** | Key talent exodus (top management + critical engineers) | 30–35% | Retention agreements pre-signing; integration roles for key target executives |
| **Talent** | Wrong leadership selection (political vs. merit-based appointments) | 10–15% | Independent assessment; external benchmarking; performance-based selection criteria |
| **Execution** | Slow integration pace — paralysis by analysis | 20–25% | 100-day plan with hard deadlines; dedicated IMO; CEO weekly integration review |
| **Execution** | Integration cost overruns undermine deal economics | 15–20% | Integration budget with 15% contingency; weekly cost-tracking |
| **Customer** | Customer defection due to service disruption / price increases | 10–15% | Customer continuity guarantees; dedicated transition team; grandfathering agreements |
| **Communication** | Poor internal communication → rumor mill fills void | 35–40% | Communication cascade pre-planned; weekly updates; Q&A forums; "no-surprise" policy |
| **Governance** | Integration governance under-resourced / lacking authority | 15–20% | IMO reporting to CEO; dedicated budget; Steering Committee with decision authority |

### 6.2 Top 5 "Deadly Sins" of Integration

1. **No Integration Leader appointed pre-signing** — Waiting until closing to plan integration guarantees value leakage in first 3 months
2. **Treating culture as "soft stuff" to address later** — Culture eats strategy for breakfast; it's the hardest thing to retrofit
3. **Keeping two separate management teams indefinitely** — Dual structures breed confusion and politics; hard decisions delayed = harder decisions later
4. **Running the business and integration as separate activities** — Integration IS the business; line managers must own it, not delegate to IMO
5. **Declaring victory too early** — True integration takes 18–36 months; early "mission accomplished" kills momentum before day job fully transformed

## 8. Integration Governance Structure

### 8.1 Governance Bodies

| Body | Composition | Cadence | Mandate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Integration Steering Committee (ISC)** | CEO, CFO, CHRO, BU Presidents, IMO Lead | Weekly (first 90 days); bi-weekly thereafter | Ultimate decision authority; resource allocation; risk escalation endpoint |
| **Integration Management Office (IMO)** | Full-time Integration Lead + 5–8 dedicated team + workstream leads | Daily stand-up | Day-to-day coordination; tracking; escalation preparation |
| **Functional Workstreams** | Functional leads from both entities + IMO liaison | Weekly | Execute on functional integration plans |
| **Clean Team** (pre-close only) | Legal-walled planning team; limited membership | Weekly | Pre-close planning without violating competition law |
| **Change Network** | 1–2 champions per department / location | Monthly | Cascade communications; gather feedback; local implementation |

### 8.2 Decision Escalation Protocol

```
Level 1 — Workstream Lead Decision
     ↓ (exceeds authority / cross-functional impact)
Level 2 — IMO Lead Decision
     ↓ (exceeds budget threshold / strategic impact)
Level 3 — Integration Steering Committee Decision
     ↓ (fundamental to deal thesis / requires Board)
Level 4 — Board of Directors
```

Budget authority thresholds: Workstream Lead ≤ RMB 500K; IMO Lead ≤ RMB 5M; ISC ≤ RMB 20M.
