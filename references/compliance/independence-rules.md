# Independence Rules — Five-Separation & Independence Tests

> **Purpose:** Reference for independence assessment in IPO DD, ongoing compliance, and remediation planning.
> **Last Updated:** 2026-05

---

## 1. The Five Separations (五独立)

Per CSRC regulations, a listed company must demonstrate independence from its controlling shareholder and actual controller in five dimensions:

| # | Dimension | Chinese | Core Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Asset Independence** | 资产独立 | Company owns all assets needed for business |
| 2 | **Personnel Independence** | 人员独立 | No concurrent roles; independent HR system |
| 3 | **Financial Independence** | 财务独立 | Independent accounting, bank accounts, tax |
| 4 | **Organizational Independence** | 机构独立 | Independent organizational structure |
| 5 | **Business Independence** | 业务独立 | Independent procurement, production, sales |

---

## 2. Asset Independence (资产独立)

### Requirements
- Company has **full legal title** to all production equipment, facilities, land use rights, IP
- No **title defects**: properties registered in controlling shareholder's name must be transferred
- Production systems (工厂、车间) are physically and legally separate
- Intangible assets (patents, trademarks, software) are registered in company's name, not controlling shareholder's
- No assets shared with related parties under informal arrangements

### Common Violation Patterns
| Pattern | Example | Remediation |
|---|---|---|
| Controlling shareholder retains land/property title | Factory land still in controlling shareholder's name | Transfer title to company via capital increase or purchase |
| Shared production facilities | Same workshop floor for company and sister company | Physical separation, separate leases |
| IP owned by controlling shareholder | Patents filed in name of controlling shareholder, company uses under informal arrangement | Assignment of IP to company |
| Core equipment on informal lease | Key production line borrowed from parent without formal lease | Formal lease or purchase agreement |

### Independence Test Questions
- [ ] Can the company produce all its products using only its own assets?
- [ ] Are all land-use-rights / property titles in the company's name?
- [ ] Are all core patents registered in the company's name?
- [ ] Are there any informal asset-sharing arrangements with the controlling shareholder?
- [ ] Are there any asset pledges or liens benefiting related parties?

## 3. Personnel Independence (人员独立)

### Requirements
- **NO** senior management (GM, Deputy GM, CFO, Board Secretary) holds management position in controlling shareholder entity
- **NO** financial personnel (CFO, accounting manager, cashier) hold any position in controlling shareholder
- Directors may be nominated by controlling shareholder but must have independent fiduciary duty
- Independent HR system: recruitment, compensation, promotion, termination independent
- Employment contracts signed with the company, not the controlling shareholder
- Social insurance / housing fund contributed by the company

### Exceptions
| Role | Can be Concurrent? | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Chairman of the Board | Yes (in controlling shareholder) | Must not be CEO/GM concurrently (two roles) |
| Independent Director | No | Must be fully independent |
| GM/CEO | No | Full-time at company |
| CFO | No | Full-time at company |
| Board Secretary | No | Full-time at company |
| Supervisor | No | Full-time (worker supervisor can be employee) |

### Common Violation Patterns
| Pattern | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Company CFO also serves as controlling shareholder's deputy CFO | HIGH — must be resolved before filing |
| Company CEO is also GM of controlling shareholder's main operating subsidiary | HIGH — must resign from one |
| Company's HR manager is controlling shareholder's HR department head | MEDIUM — independence concern |
| Financial team shares office / phone line with controlling shareholder's finance dept | MEDIUM — physical separation needed |

## 4. Financial Independence (财务独立)

### Requirements
- Independent financial department and accounting system
- Separate bank accounts (no shared / cash pooling into controlling shareholder's accounts)
- Independent tax registration and filing (separate tax payer ID)
- No commingled bank accounts or cash management systems
- No financial guarantees provided for controlling shareholder
- No loans to controlling shareholder
- Internal financial management system adopted and functional
- Audit committee with independent director majority

### Common Violation Patterns
| Pattern | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Cash pooling / centralized treasury with parent company | HIGH — must terminate |
| Company pays expenses of controlling shareholder | HIGH — fund embezzlement |
| Controlling shareholder guarantees company loans → company pays nothing to controlling shareholder | LOW-Med (if properly disclosed, acceptable for parent guarantee) |
| Shared bank account for small payments | HIGH — strict prohibition |
| Accruals processed by controlling shareholder's accounting team | MEDIUM — independence issue |

---

## 5. Organizational Independence (机构独立)

### Requirements
- Company has its own board of directors, supervisory board, management
- Separate office premises from controlling shareholder
- Independent administrative departments (HR, Finance, Legal, IT)
- Independent decision-making process for significant matters
- No overlapping office locations or shared facilities

### Common Violation Patterns
| Pattern | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Company headquarters located in controlling shareholder's building without lease / separate entrance | MEDIUM — need formal lease + physical separation |
| Company's legal department = same 3 people as controlling shareholder's legal department | HIGH — personnel overlap |
| Company board and controlling shareholder board held same day, same room | LOW — but should separate formally |
| Company has no independent IT system; uses parent's ERP | MEDIUM — must license / separate |

---

## 6. Business Independence (业务独立)

### Requirements
- Company independently faces market — independent procurement, independent sales
- NO horizontal competition (同业竞争) with controlling shareholder
- NO dependency on controlling shareholder for core business functions
- Revenue from related-party sales persistent and material → independence risk

### No Horizontal Competition Test
| Test | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Same industry? | If same industry/sub-sector → competition likely |
| Same customer base? | Customer overlap → competition |
| Same geographic market? | Geographic overlap → competition |
| Same product/service? | Product overlap → competition (must resolve) |
| Different business model? | Different model within same industry → may argue no competition |

### Revenue Dependency Test
| RPT Revenue / Total Revenue | Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| <10% | Low | Acceptable with disclosure |
| 10–30% | Medium | Need reliance rationale; show alternative customers exist |
| >30% | HIGH | Must reduce OR demonstrate unavoidable + fair pricing |
| >50% | Critical | IPO likely blocked unless clear path to <30% |
| >50% from controlling shareholder | Critical | Independence fundamentally questioned |

## 7. Independence Remediation Timeline

| Remediation | Typical Duration | Timing (before filing) |
|---|---|---|
| IP transfer | 3–6 months | ≥6 months |
| Property/land transfer | 3–12 months | ≥6 months |
| Personnel separation | 1–3 months | ≥3 months |
| Financial system separation | 1–3 months | ≥3 months |
| Competitive business resolution | 6–18 months (close/sell/merge) | ≥12 months (clean year of accounts) |
| RPT reduction plan | 12–36 months | Trend must be decreasing at filing |

## 8. Anonymized Case Studies

### Case I-01: Successful Remediation (STAR Board IPO, 2023)
Company D was a spin-off from a large SOE parent. At restructuring:
- 8 patents still in parent's name → transferred via capital increase (6 months)
- CFO was seconded from parent → hired independent CFO (3 months)
- 15% of revenue from parent → reduced to 8% over 2 years
- Parent provided headquarters building under informal arrangement → signed 10-year lease
- IPO approved after 3 rounds of inquiries on independence

### Case I-02: IPO Rejected — Independence (GEM, 2023)
Company E:
- 62% of revenue from controlling shareholder's affiliates (persistent over 3 years)
- Core production patent licensed from parent with termination clause
- GM concurrently held EVP role in parent
- Financial systems shared with parent (same ERP instance)
- Even after 2 years of attempted remediation, RPT revenue still 45%
- IPO rejected: independence insufficiently demonstrated

### Case I-03: Post-IPO Enforcement (Main Board, 2024)
Company F (listed 2021):
- Post-listing investigation revealed the company had been making "advances" to controlling shareholder disguised as "prepayments"
- RMB 120M in total over 2 years — undisclosed fund embezzlement
- CSRC investigation → fine, trading suspension, controlling shareholder forced to repay
- Directors held personally liable; ICM overhaul mandated

## 9. Independence Declaration Template

Standard prospectus language:
```
发行人在资产、人员、财务、机构和业务等方面与控股股东、
实际控制人及其控制的其他企业之间完全独立，具备独立完整的
业务体系和直接面向市场独立经营的能力。

（一）资产独立：公司拥有与生产经营相关的全部资产的所有权
或使用权，不存在资产、资金被控股股东占用的情形...

（二）人员独立：公司的总经理、副总经理、财务负责人和董事会
秘书等高级管理人员均专职在公司工作并领取薪酬，未在控股股东、
实际控制人及其控制的其他企业中担任除董事、监事以外的其他职务...

... [continue for all five]
```
